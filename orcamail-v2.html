❓ How to Use OrcaMail

🐬 Welcome to OrcaMail
OrcaMail lets you send private, encrypted messages between crypto wallets on Lightchain. Only the recipient can read your message — not the server, not the network, nobody else.
🚀 Getting Started
Connect your Trust Wallet, or any EVM wallet using the Connect Wallet button. Once connected, tap Opt In — this registers your wallet so others can send you messages. Opting in is free and doesn't require a transaction.
📬 Receiving Messages
Receiving is always free. Once opted in, anyone can send you a message. Open your Inbox tab to see messages. Tap any message to read it — it decrypts right in your browser using your wallet key, so nobody else can see the content.
📤 Sending Messages
Go to the Compose tab. Enter the recipient's wallet address (0x...), a subject, and your message. Tap Send Encrypted Message — OrcaMail encrypts it so only the recipient can read it, then delivers it.

You get 5 free sends after connecting. After that, a subscription of $0.50/month in LCAI unlocks unlimited sending for 30 days.
💳 Subscribing
After your 5 free sends, go to Settings and tap Subscribe. The app calculates the current LCAI price automatically so you always pay exactly ~$0.50 USD. Approve the transaction in your wallet — subscription is active instantly once confirmed.
🔒 Your Privacy
All messages are encrypted with ECIES (the same cryptography as Ethereum) before they leave your device. The server stores only encrypted data — it cannot read your messages. Your wallet address is needed to route delivery, but message content is private.

🔐 Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2025  ·  Built on Lightchain by the OrcaMail team.

1. What OrcaMail Is

OrcaMail is a wallet-to-wallet encrypted messaging service built on the Lightchain blockchain. It is operated by an individual developer and provided as-is for the benefit of the Lightchain community.

2. What Data We Collect

When you use OrcaMail, the following data is stored on our server:

  • Wallet addresses — your Ethereum-compatible public address, used to route messages to your inbox. Wallet addresses are pseudonymous and are not linked to your real-world identity by this service.
  • Encrypted message blobs — the ciphertext of messages sent to you. These are encrypted client-side using ECIES (secp256k1 ECDH + AES-256-GCM) before they reach our server. We cannot decrypt or read your messages.
  • Public keys — your secp256k1 public key, submitted when you opt in, so senders can encrypt messages to you. This key is derived from your wallet and is not a private key.
  • Opt-in preferences — whether you have opted in to receive messages and any notification preferences you set.
  • Send counts — a per-address counter tracking how many messages you have sent, used to enforce the free-send limit.

We do not collect your name, email address, IP address logs, or any off-chain identity information.

3. End-to-End Encryption

All message content is encrypted on your device using ECIES encryption before being transmitted to our server. The server stores only the encrypted ciphertext and cannot read the contents of any message. Only the recipient, using their wallet's private key, can decrypt messages. The operator of this service has no ability to access message content.

4. Where Data Is Stored

Data is stored on a third-party cloud infrastructure provider (currently Railway). It is not stored on any personal device belonging to the operator. The cloud provider may have its own data processing terms which apply to infrastructure-level operations.

5. Data Retention & Deletion

Messages remain stored until you delete them from your inbox. Opt-in and public key records remain until you opt out. Send count records are retained for service integrity. You can remove your data at any time by opting out in the Settings tab, which deletes your opt-in record and public key from our server.

6. No Sale or Sharing of Data

We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties for marketing or advertising purposes. Data may be disclosed if required by law.
